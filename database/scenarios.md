# Help Desk Reporting Scenarios

This file lists realistic business questions that can be answered using SQL queries on the IT Help Desk Ticket Management System database.

## Scenario 1
The IT manager wants to see all open tickets with full context, including who created them, who they are assigned to, and which category they belong to.

## Scenario 2
The support lead wants to know which technician currently has the highest active workload.

## Scenario 3
Management wants to know which categories are generating the most high-priority issues.

## Scenario 4
The team wants to identify tickets that are still open but have not been assigned to any technician.

## Scenario 5
The help desk supervisor wants to find tickets that have multiple update notes, since these may represent more complex issues.

## Scenario 6
The admin wants to identify which employees submit the most tickets.

## Scenario 7
Management wants to know which categories have above-average ticket volume.

## Scenario 8
The support manager wants to see which technician has resolved the most tickets.

## Scenario 9
The team wants to identify tickets that have not received any updates.

## Scenario 10
The security lead wants to know which employees have reported the most security-related issues.

## Scenario 11
The help desk manager wants to find the current backlog by category, counting only open and in-progress tickets.

## Scenario 12
The team wants to review the full update history for every ticket, including who wrote each update.
